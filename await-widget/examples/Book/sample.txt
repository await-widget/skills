The city had been silent for three days.

Above the empty streets, thousands of drones drifted through the gray sky like mechanical birds. Their blue lights flickered against the glass towers, searching endlessly for signals that no longer existed.

Deep beneath the ruins, Elias sat alone in the control room of the underground station. The air smelled of metal and dust. Old monitors glowed faintly in the darkness.

Every evening at exactly 21:00, the radio awakened.

A voice emerged through the static.

“If anyone can hear this… humanity is still alive.”

Elias listened without moving. The message repeated every night, always calm, always distant, as though spoken from the edge of another world.

On the seventh night, the voice changed.

“Elias,” it whispered.

His blood froze.

No one should have known his name.
